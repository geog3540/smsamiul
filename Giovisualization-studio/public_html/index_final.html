Accident HotSpots Analysis

  • HotSpots

    Historical accident data is used to identify accident hotspots. Statistical approaches and GIS are used to map accident hotspots. This research identifies areas that need focused interventions and remediation to improve road safety and minimize accident frequency and severity. Traffic engineers, transportation planners, and law enforcement employ accident hotspot research to identify high-risk regions, prioritize safety upgrades, and assess road safety measures. Critical accident hotspot analysis helps identify high-accident areas and establish mitigation solutions. This analysis reduces traffic accidents and deaths, making drivers and pedestrians safer. Accident hotspot analysis is crucial as vehicle numbers rise. It helps transportation planners and policymakers prioritize resources for problem locations. Transportation officials can improve road safety and reduce accidents by identifying accident causes such bad infrastructure, driver behavior, and environmental conditions. Thus, accident hotspot analysis makes roadways safer and communities healthier.

  • Data

    To conduct the accident hotspot mapping analysis Iowa DOT's accident location data for Johnson County was collected for 2013 to 2022. There was 23,000+ accidents were reported within the location at diffent form, for example, property damage, severe injury, minor injury, etc.
